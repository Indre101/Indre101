While doing research I came across Hitchcock font which was created by designer Matt Terich as an homage to the iconic lettering that so often appeared in Bassâ€™ title work. But I could not find the same style availabe either to implement through link or by downloading it. So I chose few fonts that look similar to the original typeface, that I used in my Project document.

Vertigon is the typeface for the main headings and in case it does not load I have imported a google font, which I thought also is very similar to the Vertigon called Poor sans. So I really wanted to keep the autheticity of the Saul Bass style, since his typography was a big part of his movie poster designs.

For body text, captions and other text I used Helvetica, since it fits in the same sans serif typeface family as others and it is easily readible. Also the clear and simple style of Helvetica compliments well the other used typefaces.

I used relative font size unit em, for better adaptation when resizing the viewport. The sizes vary from 0.8 for captions and short text to the headings where is size is 2em.

His unique, paper -cut lettering styles also give the posters movement and tension. -Joyce Yin .
