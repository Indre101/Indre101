Saul Bass

Saul Bass was an American graphic designer and Academy Award-winning filmmaker, best known for his design of motion-picture title sequences, film posters, and corporate logos. During his 40-year career Bass worked for some of Hollywood's most prominent filmmakers, including Alfred Hitchcock, Otto Preminger, Billy Wilder, Stanley Kubrick and Martin Scorsese.

“Bass’s work is a great example of less may be better. Good design doesn’t need to throw as much information as it can at the audience; sometimes, focusing attention on one element can strengthen the impact rather than overwhelm it.” -Kasia Piekut

Saul Bass worked in advertisement as well, but he is mostly known though not through his work in advertising, but his graphic artwork for movie posters and the famous reinvention of movie title credits. Titles became a spectacle to be seen. Film reels with Bass credits were delivered to movie theaters along with a note: “projectionist – pull curtain before titles.”

“Bass stepped up the sophistication of movie posters with distinctive minimal style and he completely revolutionized the role of title credits in films.”-Alex Bigman
